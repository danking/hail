Hail

Introduction for Scientists

Hail is a Python library for the interactive analysis of genotype, whole-exome-sequencing, and whole-gneome-sequencing datasets of any size. Hail can import many genetics formats including VCF, UKB BGEN, and PLINK. Hail enables variant and sample selection based on arbitrary sample, variant, allele, or genotype statistics. Hail supports linear, logistic and poisson regression.

Hail annotates. The Hail team maintains many annotation datasets in native Hail format for low-effort annotation onto your genetic datasets. One line of Hail can load ten different genetic annotations. Analysts are freed from finding, harmonize, or importing data. The data is simply annotated where it belongs.

Hail scales. Hail can leverage clusters of a thousand machines to analyze data quickly. Fast computation allows rapid iteration on QC and regression parameters leading to higher quality results.

Hail adapts. In addition to the standard VCF-like datatypes, Hail can also work with gene-sample, gene-population, gene-phenotype, and variant-phenotype datasets.

Hail matrix multiplies. Hail enables the calculation of huge matrix multiplies, such as a banded LD Matrix of tens of millions of variants or a pruned relationship matrix of hundreds of thousands of samples.
